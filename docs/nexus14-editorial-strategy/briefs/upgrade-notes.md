## 1. Car insurance page — `/best-car-insurance-for-foreign-drivers-and-international-students-.../-4/` (post 48682)
**Add:**
- "Cheapest for new immigrants" ranked table — columns: `Insurer | Avg. monthly premium for a
  new driver record | Accepts foreign license | Accepts ITIN | SR-22 availability if needed`.
  Premium figures must be sourced per-insurer at write time (rate quotes or published averages,
  dated) — do not estimate.
- New H2: "**Why US insurers rate you as a brand-new driver**" — explains that US insurers
  generally cannot see/verify foreign driving history, so a foreign national with 15 years of
  accident-free driving abroad is typically rated the same as a first-time driver in the US.
  This is a real, non-obvious pain point worth its own section, not a footnote.

## 2. Health insurance page — `/health-insurance-for-new-immigrants-usa/` (post 47162) — confirm exact post ID before editing, not verified live in this session
**Add:**
- Green-card / LPR-specific ACA section: Lawful Permanent Residents are eligible for ACA
  Marketplace coverage **and** premium tax credits immediately (no waiting period) — this is
  a common point of confusion worth its own clearly-labeled section.
- Medicaid 5-year bar explainer: most LPRs face a 5-year waiting period for federal Medicaid
  eligibility — but **refugees and asylees are exempt** from this bar. State this distinction
  explicitly; conflating "immigrant" as one uniform Medicaid-eligibility category is a common
  and consequential competitor error.
- Both additions need direct citation to HealthCare.gov / CMS guidance — do not paraphrase
  from secondary sources on anything eligibility-related, this is exactly the kind of claim
  that causes real harm if wrong.
