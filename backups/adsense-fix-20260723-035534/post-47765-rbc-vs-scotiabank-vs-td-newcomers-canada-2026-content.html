RBC vs Scotiabank for Newcomers: Detailed Verdict

Scotiabank wins on total value and newcomer-specific features. The 24-month fee waiver is the decisive advantage — RBC only offers 12 months. The free international money transfers for 2 years is also unique to Scotiabank and saves $25–$40 per transfer if you regularly send money abroad.

RBC wins on travel rewards and premium banking. The RBC Avion Visa is one of Canada’s best travel credit cards with no credit history required. If you’re a PR holder who travels frequently, the Avion points system (especially for Business Class redemptions) can easily outvalue Scotiabank’s Scene+ points over 2–3 years.

⚡ Decision Framework: RBC vs Scotiabank

Choose Scotiabank if: you arrived recently and want to maximize savings in year 1-2 | you send money internationally regularly | you want the longest fee waiver

Choose RBC if: you travel internationally for work or pleasure | you value premium travel rewards | you want a longer-term premium banking relationship

RBC vs TD for Newcomers: Detailed Verdict

RBC wins over TD for most newcomers due to the 12-month fee waiver (vs. TD’s 6 months) and the superior RBC Avion Visa vs. TD Cash Back Visa. However, TD wins if you frequently travel between Canada and the USA — TD Bank operates as TD Bank N.A. in the United States, and you can link Canadian and US accounts for easier cross-border transfers.

Scotiabank vs BMO for Newcomers

BMO offers the highest advertised cash bonus ($350) but has critical disadvantages: you can’t open a BMO account online before arriving in Canada (requires in-branch), and the conditions for the full $350 bonus are more complex. Scotiabank’s 24-month fee waiver (vs. BMO’s 12 months) plus the ability to open online before arriving gives Scotiabank the edge for most newcomers.

For International Students: Which Bank is Best?

International students in Canada have specific needs: low minimum balance requirements, digital-first banking, and accounts that work with their study permit. All 5 major banks offer student accounts, but for newcomer international students specifically:

  • If you prefer service in Hindi, Mandarin, or Cantonese: CIBC and BMO have strong multilingual staffing in major Canadian cities, with competitive student account pricing.
  • If you prefer service in Spanish: Scotiabank has Spanish-speaking staff at many branches.
  • If you send money internationally often: Scotiabank and RBC are widely available in cities with large newcomer communities and offer international transfer options — compare current transfer fees before choosing, and if your transfer corridor is subject to international sanctions, confirm with your bank which countries and payment methods are permitted before attempting a transfer.

FAQ: RBC vs Scotiabank vs TD for Newcomers

Is RBC or Scotiabank better for newcomers to Canada?

Scotiabank is generally better for newcomers to Canada in 2026 due to its 24-month fee waiver (vs. RBC’s 12 months), free international money transfers for 2 years, and higher cash bonus (up to $300 vs. RBC’s $200). However, RBC is better if you travel internationally and value the RBC Avion Visa’s premium travel rewards. For newcomers focused on saving money in their first 2 years, Scotiabank’s StartRight™ program generally delivers more total value thanks to the longer fee-free period — confirm current offer terms directly with each bank before deciding.

Is TD or Scotiabank better for newcomers Canada?

Scotiabank is better than TD for most newcomers due to the 24-month free banking period (vs. TD’s 6 months) and free international wire transfers. However, TD is better if you frequently travel between Canada and the US, as TD Bank operates in both countries, allowing seamless cross-border account linking. Both offer similar cash bonuses (up to $300), but Scotiabank’s overall package generally offers more value than TD’s, primarily due to the longer fee waiver and free transfers — verify current terms with each bank.

Which is the best big bank for newcomers in Canada?

Scotiabank is the best big bank for newcomers in Canada in 2026 based on total package value, program accessibility, and newcomer-specific features. It offers 2 years of free banking, up to $300 cash bonus, 2 years of free international money transfers, a credit card with no credit history required, and the ability to open online before arriving in Canada. Among the Big 5 banks, RBC is the second-best for newcomers who prioritize travel rewards, and BMO offers the highest raw cash bonus ($350) with more conditions.

RBC vs BMO for new immigrants Canada — which is better?

For most new immigrants, RBC is slightly better than BMO due to the ability to open an account online before arriving in Canada (BMO requires in-branch visit), and RBC’s travel rewards (Avion Visa) are superior to BMO’s offering. However, BMO has the higher cash bonus ($350 vs. RBC’s $200) and the same 12-month fee waiver. BMO is particularly strong for newcomers in Toronto and Vancouver who can visit a branch with Mandarin/Cantonese staff.
